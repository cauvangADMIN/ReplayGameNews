In **God of War (2018)** and **God of War Ragnarök**, prophecy plays a powerful role in shaping the story.

The giants of Jötunheim left behind murals predicting events long before they happened.

![](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-1.jpg)

These murals reveal something shocking.

Kratos and Atreus’ entire journey was **foretold long before it began**.

But hidden within those prophecies lies an even darker secret.

A prophecy that suggests the fate of Kratos…

And the true destiny of Atreus, also known as **Loki**.

- - -

## The Giants Who Could See the Future

![Jotunheim prophecy wall](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-2.jpg)

The giants of Norse mythology possess a rare ability.

They can see fragments of the future.

Using this knowledge, they carved massive murals that depict important events across time.

When Kratos and Atreus finally reach **Jötunheim**, they discover one of these murals.

What they see is shocking.

The wall shows scenes from their own journey.

* their meeting with Freya  
* their battle with Baldur  
* their travel across the Nine Realms  

Everything that happened during their adventure had already been predicted.

But the most disturbing part of the prophecy was hidden behind a curtain.

- - -

## The Mural That Predicted Kratos’ Death

![Kratos death prophecy mural](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-3.jpg)

Behind the hidden section of the mural lies a chilling image.

It appears to show **Kratos dying in Atreus’ arms**.

A strange magical energy flows from Kratos’ mouth into Atreus’ body.

The implication is terrifying.

Atreus might one day witness the death of his own father.

Kratos sees the mural. But he chooses not to tell Atreus about it.

Instead, he quietly accepts the possibility that his fate might already be written.

For someone who has fought against gods and destiny before…

This prophecy feels disturbingly inevitable.

- - -

## Atreus Learns His True Name

![Atreus Loki reveal scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-4.jpg)

The mural reveals another shocking truth.

Atreus is not just Kratos’ son. His giant name is **Loki**.

The giants had been waiting for Loki’s arrival for generations.

According to the prophecy, Loki would play a crucial role in the coming events of **Ragnarök**, the end of the Norse world.

This revelation changes everything. Atreus is no longer just a boy learning about the world.

He is a figure connected to destiny itself. And that destiny might involve the death of his father.

- - -

## The Prophecy That Drives Ragnarök

![Ragnarok battle prophecy scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-5.jpg)

The prophecy does more than predict Kratos’ death. It also describes the coming of Ragnarök.

The death of Baldur triggers **Fimbulwinter**, a three-year winter that signals the beginning of the apocalypse. 

From that moment forward, events begin moving toward the final war between gods and giants.

Kratos and Atreus spend much of **God of War Ragnarök** trying to understand this prophecy.

More importantly, they try to avoid it.

Because if the prophecy is correct… Kratos might not survive the coming war.

- - -

## Can Fate Be Changed?

![Kratos and Atreus discussion scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-6.jpg)

One of the central themes of God of War Ragnarök is the idea of fate.

Are people truly bound to destiny? Or can they choose a different path?

The Norns — powerful beings who see the future — explain something surprising.

Prophecy doesn’t necessarily force events to happen.

Instead, it predicts how people are **likely to act based on their nature**.

For example, Kratos was once known for responding to every conflict with violence.

That pattern made his future easy to predict.

But if Kratos changes who he is… The prophecy can change as well.

- - -

## The Choice That Breaks the Prophecy

![Kratos sparing Thor scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-7.jpg)

During the final battle of Ragnarök, Kratos faces Thor.

In the past, Kratos would have killed him without hesitation.

But this time, something different happens. Kratos chooses mercy.

He spares Thor’s life and tries to end the cycle of vengeance.

This single decision breaks the pattern the Norns predicted.

And because of that change… The prophecy begins to unravel.

Kratos does not die during Ragnarök as the mural once suggested.

For the first time in his life, Kratos has truly changed his fate.

- - -

## The Hidden Final Prophecy

![Kratos final mural worship scene](IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER)

Near the end of Ragnarök, Kratos discovers another secret mural hidden behind Atreus’ prophecy.

This mural shows a completely different future.

Instead of being feared as a destroyer of gods… Kratos is shown as a **hero worshiped by mortals**.

People gather around him in gratitude. They see him not as a monster… But as a protector.

For a man who spent his entire life believing he was beyond redemption, this prophecy is almost unbelievable.

Kratos finally realizes something. His story does not have to end in tragedy.

- - -

## The Destiny of Loki

![Atreus leaving journey scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-8.jpg)

At the end of Ragnarök, Atreus sets out on his own journey.

His goal is to find the remaining giants and help them rebuild their people.

This is the beginning of Loki’s true path. Not as a destroyer.

But as someone who might help restore balance between the realms.

For the first time, both father and son step beyond the destiny others predicted for them.

- - -

## Final Thoughts

The prophecy of Kratos and Loki is one of the most fascinating mysteries in the God of War series.

At first, it seemed like fate was unavoidable.

Kratos would die.

Atreus would fulfill his destiny.

And Ragnarök would destroy everything.

But the story ultimately delivers a different message. Fate is not always a prison.

![](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/loki-9.jpg)

Sometimes it is simply a reflection of who we are. And when people change…

The future can change with them. That is the true secret behind the prophecy of Kratos and Loki.
