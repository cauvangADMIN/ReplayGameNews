In **God of War (2018)**, Baldur appears as a violent and unstable villain.

![](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-1.jpg)

He fights Kratos without fear.\
He laughs while being brutally injured.\
And he seems obsessed with finding a way to finally feel something.

But what many players don’t realize is that Baldur wasn’t always like this.

In fact, the reason Baldur hates his own mother, **Freya**, reveals one of the most tragic stories in the entire God of War series.

Because Baldur’s greatest enemy was never Kratos. It was the curse his own mother placed on him.

- - -

## The Son of Freya

![Freya and Baldur lore scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-2.jpg)

Baldur is the son of **Freya**, the powerful Vanir goddess of magic, and **Odin**, the ruler of Asgard. Freya loved her son deeply.

In fact, she loved him so much that she became terrified of losing him.

At some point, Freya learned of a prophecy predicting Baldur would die a meaningless death.

For a mother who had already suffered through Odin’s cruelty and manipulation, the idea of losing her child was unbearable.

So Freya decided to do something drastic. She used her powerful Vanir magic to place a spell on Baldur.

A spell meant to protect him forever. But the spell came with a terrible cost.

- - -

## The Curse That Ruined Baldur’s Life

![Baldur cannot feel pain scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-3.jpg)

Freya’s spell made Baldur **completely invulnerable**.

No weapon could harm him.\
No magic could damage him.\
Nothing in the Nine Realms could kill him.

At first, this seemed like a blessing.

Baldur could never die. But there was a hidden side effect.

The spell also removed **all physical sensation**. Baldur couldn’t feel pain.

But he also couldn’t feel:

* warmth  
* cold  
* taste  
* touch  
* pleasure

He couldn’t even feel the wind on his skin. Freya had made her son immortal.

But she had also made him **unable to experience life itself**.

- - -

## Living in a Sensory Prison

![Baldur emotional rage scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-4.jpg)

Imagine living in a world where you can’t feel anything.

No warmth from sunlight.

No taste of food.

No comfort from another person’s touch.

For Baldur, existence became a living prison.

He wandered the Nine Realms searching for a way to break the curse. But Freya refused to remove it.

She believed the curse was the only thing keeping him alive. And in Baldur’s eyes, that meant one thing.

His mother cared more about **her fear of losing him** than about his suffering.

Over time, Baldur’s frustration turned into rage. Then that rage turned into hatred.

- - -

## Baldur’s Obsession With Feeling Pain

![Baldur fighting Kratos scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-7.jpg)

When Baldur first encounters Kratos in the game, something strange happens.

Kratos punches him.

Hard.

And Baldur laughs.

Why?

Because even the smallest sensation feels like a miracle to him.

For someone who has lived centuries without feeling anything, pain becomes the closest thing to being alive.

That’s why Baldur keeps provoking fights.

That’s why he relentlessly hunts Kratos.

He isn’t just trying to win.

He’s trying to **feel something again**.

Even if that feeling is pain.

- - -

## The One Weakness Freya Tried to Hide

![Mistletoe arrow Atreus scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-6.jpg)

Freya’s magic was incredibly powerful. But like many spells in mythology, it had a hidden weakness.

There was one material that could break the curse. **Mistletoe.**

Freya believed the plant was too harmless to matter, so she never included it in the spell’s protection.

Unfortunately for Baldur… Atreus unknowingly carries mistletoe arrows during the journey.

During the final battle, Baldur is pierced by one of those arrows.

And suddenly, after centuries of numbness… The curse breaks.

Baldur finally feels pain again. For the first time in hundreds of years.

- - -

## The Moment Baldur Finally Feels Something

![Baldur feeling pain scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-5.jpg)

When Baldur realizes the curse is gone, he experiences something incredible.

Pain. Real physical pain. But instead of fear… He feels relief.

Finally, he can feel the world again. The wind. The cold.

The warmth of blood running through his body. For Baldur, pain isn’t a curse anymore.

It’s freedom. But the moment quickly turns tragic.

Because the first person he blames for his centuries of suffering…

Is standing right in front of him. His mother.

- - -

## Why Baldur Tried to Kill Freya

![Baldur attacking Freya scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-10.jpg)

After the curse breaks, Baldur doesn’t thank Freya. He attacks her. In his mind, Freya stole his life from him.

She forced him to exist for centuries in a world where nothing had meaning.

And she refused to remove the curse even after he begged her.

To Baldur, Freya didn’t save him. She imprisoned him.

That’s why Baldur tries to kill his own mother during the final battle.

And in one of the most tragic moments in the series… Kratos is forced to intervene.

Kratos kills Baldur to save Freya. Ending the life of a god who had suffered for centuries.

- - -

## The Tragic Irony of Baldur’s Story

![Freya mourning Baldur scene](https://pub-da217e9da43e4b3ba192844eb749f8ad.r2.dev/baldur-8.jpg)

The tragedy of Baldur’s story is painfully ironic. Freya cast the spell because she loved her son.

She wanted to protect him from death. But that protection destroyed their relationship.

Baldur grew to hate the person who loved him most. And in the end, Freya watched her son die anyway.

Everything she tried to prevent… Still happened.

- - -

## Final Thoughts

Baldur is often remembered as the villain of **God of War (2018)**.

But when you look deeper into his story, he becomes something else entirely.

A victim of fear.

A prisoner of love.

A god who spent centuries searching for the simple ability to feel alive.

And that’s why Baldur’s hatred for his mother isn’t just anger.

It’s the tragic result of a mother who tried to protect her son… And accidentally ruined his life.
